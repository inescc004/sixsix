A research project exploring digital drawing as a means of expression for patients with locked-in syndrome.

Drawing for social
RE - Connectivity

The project uses the human body as an interface, converting minimal physical movements into drawing commands.

This approach recognizes that creative expression is a fundamental human need, particularly important for those with severe motor impairments.

Events

SiX will host workshops and presentations for students, researchers, and healthcare professionals. These events will focus on:

Understanding LIS and its impact

Raising awareness of LIS and the challenges faced by patients and their families.
